Stew Recipe

Description

Irish stew is more commonly made with lamb in Ireland. It's easy to prep and then let it cook slowly until the meat and vegetables are fall-apart tender and the broth is full of flavor. Every Irish household has its own recipe for this comforting dish and this is my mom's. It's perfect on a cold day or on St. Patrick's Day served with a pint of Guinness, of course!

Ingredients

  • oil
  • Lamb Shoulder
  • Seasonings
  • Vegetables
  • Water

Steps

  1. Cook the lamb pieces until evenly browned, then season with salt and pepper.
  2. Add the onion, carrots, and parsnips, then stir in the water. Cover and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce the heat and simmer until lamb is tender.
  4. Stir in the potatoes and simmer, then add the leeks and rosemary.
  5. Continue to simmer uncovered, until potatoes are tender.
  6. Serve garnished with parsley.
